Overview

There are 10 homework assignments in this course. Your lowest 2 scores will be dropped and the remaining 8 will all be equally weighted while computing your final grade.

Each assignment will be a programming/development assignment with a submission of your software and documentation on AutoLab, though there will be no automated grading in this course.

The following apply to all homework assignments except where exceptions are explicitly stated:

  • You may choose any programming language you prefer
  • No external libraries or frameworks are allowed (TCP and concurrency exception below for certain languages)
  • Any libraries/packages/classes/functions/etc included with your language of choice that completes part of an assignment - related to the course content - for you are not allowed. This includes any and all HTTP libraries
  • Code included with your language of choice that handles TCP socket servers and concurrency are allowed. Most assignments will start with a TCP socket server upon which you'll develop a web server. Industry standard external library for TCP and concurrency are allowed if they are common in the community for your language (Ex. Akka for Scala).

Documentation

Each homework must be accompanied by report. This report will serve as documentation for the course staff to understand your code during grading. At a minimum your reports must include:

  • How to run your software
    • After downloading the zip file with your code, list all steps needed to run your software. Include any important details such as specific compiler versions, build tools required, services that need to be running, etc. You can assume your audience is technically minded
    • Ex. It's fine to say "run gcc on server.c" or "run server.py with python3" without further explanation, but your instructions cannot be ambiguous. Saying "Compile server.c" or "run server.py" is not acceptable since you might have tested with a different compiler/interpreter than the course staff will run
  • How to test your software
    • This must include step-by-step instructions containing everything the course staff needs to confirm that you've completed the objectives of the assignment
    • Ex. Include the paths to your endpoints, testing procedures, expected behaviour
    • The simplest way to write this section is to write out the steps you took to verify that your software completed the objectives
  • The architecture of your software
    • Describe how you completed the objectives of the assignment, how your software is structured, and why you made the design decisions you made. A reader should have a good understanding of your software by reading this section without looking at a single line of your code

These reports will guide the course staff while grading your assignments. Note that it is possible for you to complete the programming part of an objective and not earn full credit due to poor documentation and organization. Specific grading decisions will be made the course staff on a case-by-case basis.

Submission

Each homework assignment is submitted in three parts:

  • Software
    • All of the code and files that comprise your software submitted on AutoLab in a single zip file
  • documentation
    • A single txt document meeting the requirements listed above
  • Self-Evaluation Form
    • Fill out a form reporting how much of the assignment you successfully completed
    • To ensure timely grading without automated grading: If the course staff does not grade your homework submission within 1 week of the deadline, you will earn the grade you self-reported via this form.
    • Intentionally falsifying this form is an academic integrity violation.
